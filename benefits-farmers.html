Turning Residue Into Income

Farmers work hard every season and still face rising input costs, unpredictable weather, and tight margins. Crop residue is often treated as waste — burned to clear fields or left unused.

By collecting and processing this residue, we create a simple way for farmers and FPOs to earn extra income without changing their farming practices.

What You Can Expect

Extra income from crop residue

Turn leftover stalks, husk, and other waste into an additional income source instead of burning it. This is not crop-level pricing — it’s bonus income from material that normally has no market value. Even a small amount becomes meaningful when collected properly.

Flexible benefit options

We understand every farmer’s needs are different, so we provide flexibility. You can choose between direct payment or a barter option such as biochar or soil enhancers. Only one option is chosen per transaction to keep things clear and simple. The goal is to support your fields in the way that helps you the most.

Cleaner fields and faster preparation

Removing residue properly helps prepare land for the next crop cycle without smoke or delays.

Better air for your village & Long-term trust

Avoiding stubble burning helps protect the air quality your community depends on. We value steady relationships and transparent communication — not one-time collection.

Types of Biomass We Accept

We work with a wide range of crop residues. If you’re unsure whether your material qualifies, we will guide you through moisture levels, storage, and suitability.

  • Paddy straw
  • Sugarcane trash / Bagasse
  • Groundnut shells
  • Cotton stalks
  • Maize stalks
  • Sunflower waste
  • Coconut shells / husk
  • Any dry crop residue suitable for briquetting

Support for FPOs and Cooperatives

FPOs can play an important role in organizing biomass supply from multiple farmers. We offer:

  • Bulk pricing
  • Pickup planning
  • Long-term purchase agreements
  • Technical support on moisture and storage
  • Clear expectations on volume

FPOs can earn additional revenue by coordinating and consolidating supply.

Good for Your Fields. Good for Your Community.

Selling crop residue instead of burning it helps:

  • Reduce local air pollution
  • Protect soil health
  • Improve living conditions for families
  • Support sustainable farming practices

Cleaner air benefits everyone — especially your own village.
